Come and find out more about the Quantum Secrets of Photosynthesis at the Royal Society Summer Science Exhibition 2016 from 4th-10th July!

In the initial stages of photosynthesis, energy is captured from sunlight by specialised molecules called chromophores, which form light harvesting antennae inside the cells of photosynthetic organisms. These antennae absorb photons and then transfer the energy to a reaction centre where it can be converted to a chemically reactive state. It turns out that the capture and transfer of energy are enhanced by three weird quantum secrets to help make the most of the energy available. Check out our video below to find out more...
